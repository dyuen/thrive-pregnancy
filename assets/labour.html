Labour and Birth FAQ

When do I call the doctor?

A doctor is always on call for you at BC Women's Hospital. Call and ask for the doctor on call for your doctor.

604-875-2161

If you're really worried, call 911 and ask foran ambulance to take you to BC Women's Hospital.

Call your doc if:

  • You're bleeding like a period
  • Your baby isn't moving as much as usual
  • Your water breaks with or without contractions
  • You're having more than six contractions in an hour
  • You're worried
  • You need medication

How long is labour?

Some labours take a few hours, some take a few days. Both are normal. The average is 12 hours.

What helps with the pain?

  • Warm water: a tub or a shower
  • Friends and family
  • A doula
  • Massage
  • Moving
  • Dancing
  • Music
  • Laughing Gas
  • IM Morphine
  • Epidural

When do I go to the hospital?

Call the doctor to help you decide.

604-875-2161

What happens if I go to the hospital too early?

You might go home again.

Who can come with me to the hospital?

Anyone you like.

Do I get an IV in labour?

If you get an epidural, yes. An IV is a treatment for some labour complications. In a normal labour without an epidural, you won't need an IV.

What are my chances of having a Caesarian?

If your pregnancy is healthy, about one in ten.

Can I eat during labour?

Yes.

Do women in labour throw up a lot?

Yes.

Will I poop during the birth of my baby?

Yes.

What if I'm too tired to push my baby out?

You can rest a little while your body gets the strength to do it.
